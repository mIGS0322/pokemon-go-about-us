What is Pokémon GO?

Pokémon GO is a free smartphone app that merges gaming with the real world, creating an "augmented reality" experience where players catch and train Pokémon. In this game, users explore the real world as Pokémon characters appear on the in-game map based on their location. By throwing Poké Balls at these Pokémon, players aim to collect as many different characters as possible. The game features Poké Stops at real-life locations where players can gather free Poké Balls and other items. Additionally, players can find Pokémon eggs that hatch into new creatures for their collection.

Pokémon GO is the brainchild of Niantic, Inc., developed in collaboration with Nintendo and The Pokémon Company Niantic, a pioneer in augmented reality technologies, brought the Pokémon universe to life in the game, allowing players to catch, train, and battle with virtual Pokémon in the real world. The collaborative effort between Niantic, Nintendo, and The Pokémon Company led to the launch of Pokémon GO on July 6, 2016, which quickly became a global sensation This innovative game invites players to explore their surroundings to discover and interact with Pokémon characters, blending the virtual world with reality in an engaging and interactive way.
